# Best Retro MMORPGs You Can Play on Mobile in 2026

The golden age of MMORPGs gave us some incredible games. The good news? Many of them are now playable on your phone. Here are the best retro MMORPGs you can play on mobile right now.

## 1. Tibia (via Minibia)

**Originally released:** 1997
**How to play on mobile:** [Minibia.com](https://minibia.com) — browser-based, no download

Tibia is one of the oldest MMORPGs still running. It features a massive 2D world, [four unique vocations](/wiki/vocations), a complex economy, and some of the most challenging content in any MMO. Minibia brings the full experience to your mobile browser with touch controls — no app required. See our [beginner guide](/guide) to get started.

**Why it's great on mobile:**
- Runs in any browser, no download needed
- 2D graphics perform perfectly on any phone
- Turn-based movement works well with touch controls
- Deep gameplay you can enjoy in short or long sessions

## 2. Old School RuneScape

**Originally released:** 2001 (OSRS version from 2013)
**How to play on mobile:** Official app on iOS and Android

RuneScape's old-school version has an excellent official mobile app. It's the same game as desktop with a mobile-optimized interface. The skilling system works surprisingly well on touchscreens.

**Why it's great on mobile:**
- Official app with full cross-play
- Skill grinding is perfect for mobile sessions
- Massive community and regular updates

## 3. Albion Online

**Originally released:** 2017
**How to play on mobile:** Official app on iOS and Android

While newer than the others on this list, Albion Online has a retro-inspired aesthetic and old-school sandbox design. Full loot PvP, player-driven economy, and classless combat system.

**Why it's great on mobile:**
- Gathering and crafting work well on mobile
- Cross-platform with desktop players
- Isometric view similar to classic MMORPGs

## 4. Dofus Touch

**Originally released:** 2004 (Touch version 2016)
**How to play on mobile:** Official app on iOS and Android

Dofus is a French tactical MMORPG with turn-based combat on a grid. The touch version is well-adapted for mobile with the same strategic depth as the original.

**Why it's great on mobile:**
- Turn-based combat is ideal for touchscreens
- No need for fast reflexes or precise aim
- Beautiful 2D art style

## 5. Ragnarok Origin

**Originally released:** 2002 (Origin version 2020)
**How to play on mobile:** Official app on iOS and Android

A mobile remake of the classic Ragnarok Online. It preserves the charming sprite-based art style and class system while adding quality-of-life features for mobile play.

**Why it's great on mobile:**
- Nostalgic art style
- Auto-battle system for casual grinding
- Class system with deep builds

## Why Retro MMORPGs Work on Mobile

Classic MMORPGs are actually a perfect fit for mobile devices:

- **2D/isometric graphics** run smoothly on any hardware
- **Turn-based or deliberate pacing** doesn't require twitch reactions
- **Grinding gameplay** is ideal for short mobile sessions
- **Social features** (chat, guilds, trading) work great on phones

Any of these games are worth trying if you miss the golden age of MMORPGs. The best part is that most of them are free to start, so you can sample a few and see which one hooks you.
